# Motivation

## Who I am

I'm Pietro Mondini, a Computer Science graduate from Università degli Studi di Milano (2024/25).
My thesis focused on modeling a task offloading environment using DRL, I built a simulation environment using SimPy and Gymnasium
and later trained a PPO agent via Tianshou to optimize task scheduling under latency and energy constraints.
That work gave me hands-on experience designing agents that act in simulated environments, tuning learning pipelines,
and thinking carefully about how to represent state, actions, and reward structure.

I'm comfortable in Python and with object-oriented design; I've worked with NumPy, YAML-based configuration systems, and experiment reproducibility workflows.

## Why Mesa

I came across Mesa through the GSoC 2026 listings, and the Behavioral Framework project immediately resonated with my background.
My thesis work lives at the intersection of agents, simulation, and learning, which is exactly the territory this project explores.
Reading the open discussions about continuous states, tasks with duration, and a unified behavioral framework made it clear that 
Mesa has a huge potential from a modeller pov.

## What I want to learn

I want to go deeper on agent-based modeling as a discipline the design patterns, the theoretical frameworks (BDI, needs-based architectures, reinforcement learning agents),
and how different ABM platforms have solved the same problems. 

Beyond the conceptual side, I want to develop the habit of contributing to a mature open-source codebase: reading and understanding existing architecture 
and help wherever possible to improve it, writing code not just for the sake of writing code, and modeling interesting and challenging problems and systems.

I'm also curious to understand where Mesa's current abstractions are genuinely expressive and where they force awkward workarounds that kind of comparative, 
critical analysis is something I haven't done at the framework level before.

## Where I want to go

At the moment, I'm an M.Sc. student and my ambitions for the future are either to work on a PhD or to pursue a career in AI. 
Mesa would be a natural fit for me to get my hands on an open-source project that I can contribute to.
